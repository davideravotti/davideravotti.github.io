Teichmüller Dynamics Reading Group



Meetings usually take place at 2pm on Tuesdays in the second floor seminar room in Howard House.

We are using the following material:

  • Introduction to Teichmüller theory and its applications to dynamics of interval exchange transformations, flows on surfaces and billiards, G. Forni and C. Matheus, Journal of Modern Dynamics, 8(3-4), 271-436, 2014.
  • A Primer on Mapping Class Group, B. Farb and D. Margalit, Princeton Univeristy Press, 2011.
  • The scenery flow for geometric structures on the torus: the linear setting, P. Arnoux and A.M. Fisher, Chinese Annals of Mathematics, 22B(4), 427-470, 2001. (Preprint available here).
  • Deviation for interval exchange transformations, A. Zorich, Ergodic Theory and Dynamical Systems, 17(6), 1477-1499, 1997.
  • Flat surface models of ergodic systems, K. Lindsey and R. Trevino. (Preprint available here).
  • The visits to zero of a random walk driven by an irrational rotation, A. Avila, D. Dolgopyat, E. Duryev and O. Sarig, Israel Journal of Mathematics, 207(2), 653-717, 2015.



Short courses and special seminars

Within the reading group activity, we invited external speakers to deliver the short courses/seminars listed below.
Scientific visitors and speakers were supported by the ERC Starting Grant ChaParDyn.

Short course by Dr. Alexandra Skripchenko
National Research University Higher School of Economics (visiting from 31 January to 06 February)

  1. Novikov's problem on plane sections of periodic surfaces; exeptional chaotic behaviour.
  2. Reduction to systems of isometries, minimality.
  3. Analogous of Rauzy Induction for systems of isometries.
  4. Thermodynamic formalism and existence of a natural invariant measure on the set of admissible parameters.


Short course by Dr. Elise Goujard
Laboratoire de Mathématiques d'Orsay (visiting from 09 to 17 February)

  1. Siegel-Veech constants for strata of Abelian differentials.
  2. Diffusion rate and Lyapunov exponents for the Ehrenfest wind-tree model.
  3. Generating functions associated to counting ramified torus covers are quasimodular forms; examples for the strata H(2) and H(1,1).


Seminar by Mr. Przemysław Berk
Nicolaus Copernicus University in Torun (visiting from 07 to 14 March)

  1. Non-isomorphism criterion for flows with their inverses via joinings.
  2. Applications to suspension flows over irrational rotations.


Seminar by Dr. Kathryn Lindsey
University of Chicago (visiting from 13 to 25 March)

  1. Examples of cutting and stacking constructions and Bratteli diagrams: Hajian-Kakutani skyscraper and Pascal-adic transformations.
  2. Horocycle orbit closure of translation surfaces.


Seminar by Dr. Adam Kanigowski
PennState University (visiting from 11 to 21 May)

  1. Singular spectrum for Kochergin flows.


Seminar by Prof. Omri Sarig
Weizmann Institute of Science (visiting from 19 to 25 June)

  1. A generalisation of Beck's Theorem to rotations of bounded type.
