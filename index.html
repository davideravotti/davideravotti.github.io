Davide Ravotti

I am a postdoctoral assistant at the School of Mathematics of Monash University. I have completed my Ph.D. at the University of Bristol in 2018 under the supervision of Prof. Corinna Ulcigrai.
Here you can find my CV.

Before starting my Ph.D., I have studied some aspects of Algebraic Logic related to Equidistribution Theory.
At the moment, I study smooth dynamical systems and I am interested in ergodic and mixing properties of parabolic flows (in particular, locally Hamiltonian flows on compact surfaces, time-changes of nilflows and perturbations of unipotent flows in semisimple Lie groups), and of partially hyperbolic diffeomorphisms.

Publications and preprints

  1. Parabolic perturbations of unipotent flows on compact quotients of SL(3,R), Communications in Mathematical Physics, to appear.
  2. Mixing for suspension flows over skew-translations and time-changes of quasi-abelian filiform nilflows, Ergodic Theory and Dynamical Systems, to appear.
  3. Quantitative mixing for locally Hamiltonian flows with saddle loops on compact surfaces, Annales Henri Poincaré, 18(12):3815-3861, 2017.
  4. Measures induced by units, (joint paper with Giovanni Panti), Journal of Symbolic Logic, 78(3):886-910, 2013.
